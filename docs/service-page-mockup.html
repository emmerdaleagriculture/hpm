Paddock topping.

Flail or rotary. Grass tyres, no compaction. Same-week bookings across Hampshire.

Topping is the foundation of paddock care — a single pass cuts back rank grass, weeds, and seed heads, evens the sward, and resets the field for the next cycle of grazing or conservation. Done properly, once or twice a season is all it takes.

What paddock topping actually does

Over a grazing season, horses graze selectively — cropping the grass they like close to the ground and leaving tall, woody stems, dock, thistle, nettle, and seeded-out grass standing. The result is a patchy, uneven paddock where productive grass is outcompeted and weeds set seed for next year.

Topping cuts the whole field to an even height, typically 75–100mm. This removes the rank vegetation, prevents weeds from seeding, encourages the productive grasses to tiller and thicken, and gives you a paddock that's ready to recover before the next rotation.

Why compact machinery matters

Most contractors turn up with a 120–180 hp agricultural tractor on standard tyres. On your paddock, that means deep ruts, soil compaction, and long-term damage to drainage and grass recovery — particularly on wet ground.

We use lightweight compact tractors with wide turf tyres, specifically spec'd for sensitive ground. Minimal compaction, no ruts, and your paddock recovers faster because the soil structure hasn't been crushed.

When to book
Most paddocks need topping twice a year — once in late spring (late May / early June) to catch weeds before seeding, and again in late summer (August / early September) to reset before autumn.

What's included

  • Pre-cut walk of the paddock to identify and mark hazards (stones, debris, horse droppings that should be cleared)
  • Topping with flail or rotary mower depending on growth density and field condition
  • Even cut height across the whole paddock — no scalping on ridges, no missed patches
  • Tidying passes around fence lines, gate posts, trees, and water troughs
  • Cut material left to mulch naturally, or collected if you'd prefer (optional — see dung sweeping and flail collection)

Pricing

Every paddock is different, so quotes reflect the actual field — size, access, growth condition, and how often you'd like us to visit. Most horse-owner paddocks fall into a predictable range; we'll give you a firm figure either on the phone or after a no-cost site visit if it's a larger or more complex job.

Regular customers who book a topping schedule (spring + late summer, or quarterly) receive a reduced rate. Ask when you call.
