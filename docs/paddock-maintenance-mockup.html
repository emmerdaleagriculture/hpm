Paddock maintenance

Everything your paddock actually needs

The complete picture — what paddock maintenance involves, when each job matters, how it's priced, and how to get someone reliable doing it for you.

Paddock maintenance is the ongoing work that keeps a horse paddock healthy, safe, and grazing-ready year-round. It covers everything from cutting and harrowing through to weed control, fertiliser, and ground repair — done at the right time, in the right order, with the right kit.

Most paddocks need attention three or four times a year. Get the timing right and the field stays in condition with relatively little effort. Get it wrong — or skip a season — and the work needed to recover the field is several times what regular maintenance would have cost.

Year round

A season-by-season schedule

Paddock work is timing-led. Here's what each season usually wants — though the right schedule depends on your stocking density, soil type, and how the previous year went.

Spring Mar — May

Wake the field up. Get on top of weeds before they bolt.

  • Chain harrowing to lift thatch and spread muck
  • Rolling to firm up frost-heaved ground
  • First weed control pass — ragwort, docks, thistles
  • Spring fertiliser application
  • Overseeding bare patches
Summer Jun — Aug

Keep the grass productive. Knock back what the horses won't.

  • First topping after seed-set
  • Spot-spraying persistent weeds
  • Manure sweeping to keep parasites down
  • Possible second topping in late August
Autumn Sep — Nov

Set the field up for winter and the spring after.

  • Final topping if needed
  • Overseeding while ground is warm
  • Mole ploughing for drainage on wet fields
  • Autumn fertiliser on poorer ground
  • Ditch clearance before winter rain
Winter Dec — Feb

Heavy ground work and structural jobs while grass isn't growing.

  • Rotavating tired sections for renovation
  • Stone burying on rough ground
  • Land & ditch clearance
  • Planning the year's spray and fertiliser programme

What does it cost?

How pricing works

There's no fixed price for paddock maintenance because no two paddocks are the same. A fair quote depends on the size, the condition, the access, and what specifically wants doing. Here's roughly how it shakes out.

  • i
    Most jobs are priced per acre or per hour Cutting, harrowing, rolling, and spraying are typically per-acre. Specialist or short-duration work — small jobs, awkward access — is more often quoted by the hour.
  • ii
    Travel matters more than acreage on small jobs Two acres an hour from the yard versus two acres ninety minutes away are different jobs. I'm honest about this on the call rather than baking it into a flat rate that hurts everyone.
  • iii
    Materials are passed through at cost Fertiliser, seed, herbicide — you pay what I pay, with no markup. The only thing I charge for is the work and the kit running.
  • iv
    Bundled or contract maintenance is cheaper than one-offs Knowing in advance that I'll be on your field three times a year means I can plan routes and time-of-year, which keeps your costs down. See the contract section below.
Most jobs I can quote over the phone from a description of the field. For trickier ones — heavy renovation, drainage, jobs with awkward access — I'll come and walk it with you, no charge.
